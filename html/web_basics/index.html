Once upon a time I went to Asia.

  1. I bought a one-way ticket to Korea because I knew there would be WiFi there, and I wanted to get some writing done.
  2. Then I flew to Beijing to visit for only 72 hours. I ate a giant beetle on a stick.
  3. I arrived in the Philippines without my ATM card because I left that in the 1980s ATM machine at the Great Wall of China.
  4. After the Philippines I visited Hong Kong, where my mother's family is from. We emigrated to escape being murdered in the Cultural Revolution.
  5. Last, I visited Thailand and taught English language in K-6 classrooms in exchange for room and board.

Tips for having your quarter-life crisis abroad, should you be so lucky:

  • Don't cancel your insurance plans. Your rates will go up when you come back.
  • Get your visas months in advance, before you leave the USA.
  • Don't go "jogging" in rural Thailand. Wild dogs will chase you and bite you. Gross.
  • Visit Japan. I regret not visiting Kyoto when I was nearby.
